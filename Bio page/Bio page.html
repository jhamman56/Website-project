I was born in Mesa, Arizona. My family moved around a bit due to work opportunities for my father, living in Tuscon, Arizona, Keller, Texas, and Rio Rancho, New Mexico, before we ended up back in Arizona, living in Queen Creek. When we arrived in Queen Creek, it was the summer before I would start the seventh grade. It was here that I would finish out my education through Highschool.

I grew up in the LDS church, or Church of Jesus Christ of Ladder Day Saints. Shortly after I graduated from highschool, it came time for me to start preparing to devote two years to serve a mission for my church. I submitted my papers to receive a call that would give me my assignment for this mission, and after some time received that call. I was assigned to go to Denver, Colorado on a Spanish speaking mission, to which I dedicated the next two years of my life.

After my mission, I came back home to live with my parents. After taking it easy there for a couple months, it came time for me to get a job. During highschool I had worked at a movie theater, but now I needed to get a job that pays more than minimum wage, to start preparing myself for the future. After searching for a bit, I was able to secure a job with Wells Fargo as a bank teller. I would work here for about a year and a half.
