I was born in Mesa, Arizona. My family moved around a bit due to work opportunities for my father, living in Tuscon, Arizona, Keller, Texas, and Rio Rancho, New Mexico, before we ended up back in Arizona, living in Queen Creek. When we arrived in Queen Creek, it was the summer before I would start the seventh grade. It was here that I would finish out my education through Highschool.

I grew up in the LDS church, or Church of Jesus Christ of Ladder Day Saints. Shortly after I graduated from highschool, it came time for me to start preparing to devote two years to serve a mission for my church. I submitted my papers to receive a call that would give me my assignment for this mission, and after some time received that call. I was assigned to go to Denver, Colorado on a Spanish speaking mission, to which I dedicated the next two years of my life.

After my mission, I came back home to live with my parents. After taking it easy there for a couple months, it came time for me to get a job. During highschool I had worked at a movie theater, but now I needed to get a job that pays more than minimum wage, to start preparing myself for the future. After searching for a bit, I was able to secure a job with Wells Fargo as a bank teller. I would work here for about a year and a half.

While I was working at Wells Fargo, I also started attending Chandler Gilbert Community College to work towards a degree. Additionally, after some time had passed, my parents convinced me to make an attempt on the dating scene. I was hesitant about this, as I had, previously, had little luck when it came to dating and was content spending most of my time alone or with friends and family. I was rewarded immediately for trying, though, and met the beautiful woman who would become my wife, Danielle.

I downloaded a dating app on my phone in order to meet people, I had a few matches, but I was still too shy to actually ask someone out. While messaging Danielle, however, she mentioned that she enjoyed watching movies, and so I invited her to see the new Jumanji movie that had come out that year (2018) and we went and saw that as our first date. Movie and dinner dates occupied a large portion of our relationship, since it was apparently the best I could come up with.

After we went on several dates, we both knew that we wanted to spend the rest of our lives together. With that, I started planning out how I would propose to her. It was important to me that a lot of people were around, because I wanted it to be public how much I loved her, and with the Phoenix Comic-con coming up, the idea struck me. She was and is a fan of Disney movies, particularly the 80's and 90's era of animated movies. Also, Ming-na Wen, the voice actress of Mulan, would be doing a panel at Comic-con.
